The Device
  • Arduino Uno — main processor
  • 2× MPU-6050 IMU — upper + lower back sensors
  • Vibration motor — haptic alert output
  • HC-10 BLE — real-time wireless dashboard
  • SD card logger — offline session data
  • 3D printed case — wearable on back brace
  • ~$80 CAD in total components
The Experiment
  • 8 sessions, ~30 min each, same desk + chair
  • Sessions 1–4: vibration motor on
  • Sessions 5–8: vibration motor off (LED only)
  • Device alerts after 15° deviation for 2.5 seconds
  • Auto-calibrates to user's posture at startup
  • Data exported as CSV after each session
  • Reviewed and approved under YSC Low Risk ethics
"
Without feedback, posture collapsed to 83.6% bad — worse than the very first session with feedback. This rules out natural improvement over time. The vibration signal is the cause of improvement, not the device itself or repeated sessions alone.
Supported by published research
Ferrone et al., MDPI Sensors 2021 — dual IMU haptic feedback device showed 40–50%+ posture improvement in nurses. Same sensor methodology.
Gross et al., Spine 2006 — 83.8% of Canadians experience back pain in their lifetime; 34.2% in any given week.
Canadian Chiropractic Association, 2023 — nearly 45% of Canadians report activity limitations from chronic back pain.
